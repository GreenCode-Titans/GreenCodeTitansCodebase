    1.Sustainable and Affordable alternatives to wood charcoal and firewood.

    Wood charcoal and firewood are a main source of energy for a greater percentage of most homes. In fact, the United Nations estimates that more than 950 million people rely on wood and charcoal for cooking in Sub-Saharan Africa alone; that number will rise to 1.67 billion by 2050. They are mainly preferred as they are easily available in the market and cheaper compared to electricity or gas further to note acquiring the technologies to use them is expensive, whereas the later the technologies that support their consumption are highly accessible; indeed, firewood can be consumed without stoves, because basically materials such as stones are sufficient to balance the cooking utensils, especially in rural areas. The enduring appeal of charcoal, and its positive association with the flavor of food, continues to make it a popular choice for cooking in households.

    In this project we aim to bring to light more efficient ways of energy such as compost briquettes which are much cheaper, have low carbon emissions ,do not require cutting down of trees and can be obtained anywhere and everywhere since basically you just have to get household waste, fallen tree leaves, agricultural waste.

    More benefits that arise from briquettes are a cleaner environment as waste from households goes into better use. Creating safer jobs to the men involved in cutting of trees and burning charcoal which is sometimes done illegally. Reducing forest fires that occur due to burning of charcoal which cause great damage that takes years to reverse. Inclusion of community in safer energy sources by collecting household and agricultural waste. Cost saving as briquettes are made on uniform sizes and therefore makes it easy to count on how many one can use per combustion or per cook thus minimizing wastage.

    2.Climate Policy Council

    While each country has its set legal frameworks to govern climate change, less is known on how they conduct their duties or how they make progress on their climate advocacy.

    In this project, we advocate for the creation of a climate policy council to hold the government accountable and analyze policy set by the government and how it aligns to climate goals set by the Parliament and government. The Council will hold the government accountable through:

    • Bringing attention to societal effects of proposed policy
    • Recognizing deficient policy
    • Analyzing short- and long-term targets with climate and budget policy goals in mind
    • Promoting discussion on climate policy in society

    The climate policy council need not be within a country in limitation of financial resources. We have countries such as Sweden, Denmark, and Chile which are leading in climate policy and they could come in agreement with developing countries and hold them accountable in their going green.

    Their involvement will help in:

    • Assessing the impact of current policies in various sectors on climate objectives
    • Analyzing the societal repercussions of both established and forthcoming policies
    • Recognizing the policy domains requiring supplementary actions to align with climate targets

    3.Education and awareness

    Climate change is a global issue that needs to be addressed and acted upon for a greener future. We have all heard about it in a slogan, in an activist rally, or just a blog, but do we really know what it entails? We are all aware that our carbon emissions need to sink to zero by 2050 at the latest to stop climate change. To achieve this goal, we must radically change our lives.

    Education is crucial to promote climate action. It helps people understand and address the impacts of the climate crisis, empowering them with the knowledge, skills, values, and attitudes needed to act as agents of change.

    Here are some ways to promote climate change education and awareness:

    • **Get society on the same page.** Tell communities how they would benefit if they could plant more trees, use greener energy, and minimize printing. Sensitize people on waste management by having different color litter bins, e.g., green for recyclable waste, red for plastics, and yellow for metals and glass.
    • **Take climate change education to schools.** Teach kids how they make an impact on the environment. Implement board games and other activities that teach about climate change. Make climate change education part of the curriculum, and give incentives to students who make the most change in their environment.
    • **Advocate for companies to be on the green movement.** Encourage companies to minimize printing, use recycled materials, and reduce their carbon footprint.

    By educating and raising awareness about climate change, we can create a more sustainable future for all.
